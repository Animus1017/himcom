WELCOME TO HERITAGE INSTITUTE OF MANAGEMENT & COMMUNICATION (HIMCOM)

Best Mass Communication College in Delhi

Heritage Institute of Management and Communication (HIMCOM) is one of India’s top-ranked mass communication and journalism colleges, situated in South Delhi. Many times, awarded as the Best Mass Communication College in Delhi (INDIA) by prestigious institutions and accrediting agencies, HIMCOM aims at imparting the best quality education with real-time training experience to its students. Through this pragmatic approach, HIMCOM’s students have successfully carved out a distinct niche across the widespread media and journalism sector of the country. Since its inception in 2012, HIMCOM has catered to the media & communication world with quality talent in various domains such as anchors, journalists, writers, photographers, and radio jockeys.

The extensively professional setup, clear vision of the stewards, and tremendous experience of the faculty have always helped HIMCOM’s students in extracting the most from their courses and accomplishing career goals.

HIMCOM believes in providing the best skills to the aspirants who desire to be the part of this highly challenging area of mass communication and journalism. As mass communication and journalism require more practicalities, therefore, theoretical teaching with highly practical training is given to the students at every level using advanced and futuristic methods of knowledge and skill development.

HIMCOM campus is equipped with state-of-the-art infrastructure and ergonomics to impeccably meet with the changing demands of the mass media. A highly advanced studio, ultramodern editing laboratory, avant-garde radio studio, cutting-edge photography equipment, and a well-managed library provide excellent support to students for honing their skills in indoor as well as outdoor journalism. Further, to harness the most flourishing form of media, i.e., digital media, the Digital Media Lab of HIMCOM is an ideal platform to train every student about the various dynamics of new media and helping them to grow in the industry rather fulgently.

Is Mass Communication a good course ?

Mass Communication and Journalism are part of the Media Industry and media these days has become a huge industry around the world. Television, Radio, Newspaper, Magazines, Internet, Movies, Documentaries, etc. are all parts of the media industry. One can work with any of these after completion of their Mass Communication, Journalism, and Media course from a reputed Top Mass Communication Institutes in Delhi.
India is the home of the largest film industry in the world. Thousands of TV Channels, Radio Stations, Print Media Houses, Event Management Companies, Production Houses are flourishing and providing a vast platform for the aspirants. So the employment opportunities are really good in this sector.
Working with the media industry is not just a 9 to 5 job, it is a creative industry so it also gives a sense of creativity and achievement when your art is recognized by others and you are awarded at the National and International platforms. That feeling is something different, highly rewarding, and immensely satisfactory. The media industry not only entertains people, but it also provides them the correct information and keeps them happy as well as well informed. So all in all, working in the media industry is quite a satisfying job, and opportunities are endless. A Top Mass Communication college in Delhi like Heritage Institute of Management & Communication, South Delhi (India) can only provide you the best mass media and journalism training.

Can I do Mass Communication after 12th

Yes, you can go for a Bachelor Degree in journalism and mass communication (undergraduate course) after your 10 + 2, and further, after that, you can also go for a Master Degree in journalism and mass communication (postgraduate course) or maybe a PG Diploma in journalism and mass communication course. After 10 + 2 one can also join short-term autonomous certificate courses.

What is the salary after doing Mass Communication courses ?

Mass Communication opens lots of doors as far as employment is concerned. Salary and other perks vary depending on the profile of work you are involved with and remuneration could be anything from a few thousand to Millions.

Does Mass Communication have any scope?

Yes, very much. As we know Mass Communication is a part of the Media Industry and Media is a very powerful, widely spread, and huge industry all around the world. India itself is the home of the World’s Largest Film Industry apart from having a large number of Big Media Houses, Thousands of TV Channels, Radio Channels, Newspapers, Magazines, Web-Based Media Companies, Independent Production Houses, and Huge Corporate Media Groups. One can become a Journalist, Editor, News Reporter, Camera Person, Filmmaker, Director, Producer, Anchor, PR Professional, Event Manager, Radio Jockey, etc.

What is the duration of the Mass Communication courses ?

Masters Degree in Mass Communication & Journalism is of 2 years, consisting of 4 semesters of 6 months each. The Bachelor Degree in Mass Communication & Journalism is of 3 years, consisting of 6 semesters of 6 months each. PG Diploma in Mass Communication & Journalism is of 1 year consisting of 2 semesters of 6 months each. There are some Short Term Certificate Programs also of 6 months and 3 months duration. What is the admission process for Mass Communication courses ?

Mass Communication, Journalism, and Mass Media courses are considered professional courses and the admission process in all Best Journalism Colleges in Delhi is generally involves an aptitude test of the aspirants. For Masters Degree and PG diploma (post-graduation courses). The aspirant has to be a graduate from a reputed university with at least 50% marks and for Bachelor’s Degree (undergraduate course) an aspirant has to be a 10 + 2 pass with a minimum of 50% marks. Candidates need to apply for the desired course he or she is eligible for with the filling up of admission form of the institute. After clearing the entrance test a candidate has to go through an interactive interview session, details for which can be obtained from the admission brochure of the Institute.
